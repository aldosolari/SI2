Solution HW4

Let \(M=\{1,\ldots,m\}\) be the indexes of the null hypotheses \(H_i\) with \(p\)-values \(p_i\), \(i\in M\). Assume \(p_1,\ldots,p_m \stackrel{i.i.d}{\sim}U(0,1)\). For any \(\alpha \in (0,1]\), let \(S=\{i\in M: p_i \leq \alpha \}\) (with \(S=\emptyset\) if \(p_i> \alpha\) for all \(i \in M\)). Note that \(|S| \sim \mathrm{Binomial}(m,\alpha)\).

Bonferroni

Let \(R = \{i \in S: p_i \leq \alpha/|S|\}\) with \(R=\emptyset\) if \(S =\emptyset\). Note that \(p_i|i\in S \sim U(0,\alpha)\). Then we have \[\begin{align*} \mathrm{FWER} = & \sum_{k=1}^{m}\mathrm{P}( \cup_{i\in S} \{p_i \leq \alpha/k\}| |S|=k)\mathrm{P}(|S|=k)\\ = & \sum_{k=1}^{m} (1-(1-1/k)^k){ m \choose k }\alpha^k (1-\alpha)^{(m-k)} \end{align*}\]

Benjamini-Hochberg

Let \(R\) be the indexes of the rejections of the Benjamini-Hochberg procedure at level \(\alpha\) applied to the selected hypotheses \(H_i, i \in S\). Note that \(R=S\) and \(|S| \sim \mathrm{Binomial}(m,\alpha)\). Then we have \[\begin{align*} \mathrm{FDR} = & \mathrm{P}(S \neq \emptyset)\\ = & 1 - (1-\alpha)^m \end{align*}\]
